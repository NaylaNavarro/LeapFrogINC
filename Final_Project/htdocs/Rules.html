Rules

1. How to win: The game ends when a King is checkmated or a player resigns.

2. King: Moves one square in any direction. The King cannot move into check (a position where it can be captured).

3. Queen: Moves any number of squares in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally).

4. Rook: Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

5. Bishop: Moves any number of squares diagonally.

6. Knight: Moves in an "L" shape: two squares in one direction (horizontal or vertical) and then one square perpendicular to that direction. Knights can jump over other pieces.

7. Pawn: Moves forward one square. On its first move, a Pawn can move forward two squares. Pawns capture diagonally, one square forward to the left or right. When a Pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it can be promoted to any other piece of the player's choice, except a King.

8. Touch Rule is always active (if you click on a piece, you must move it).

9. Stalemate: If a player has no legal moves and their King is not in check, the game is a draw.
